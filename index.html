Mission Statement

Are you a fan of space, science and the Hubble Space Telescope? If yes, then this is the place you should be! Why? Because the next generation of space telescopes is upon us, and with that comes the lovely JWST. The Objective of this website is to give those interested in space science an intro to the JWST and some basic knowledge, so that you may have more interest in this topic. The choice of color on this webpage was directly inspired by the JWST in space with use of gold, silver, gray, and black. The layout uses plenty of rounded edges to give it a cleaner look as well as a black outline around most of the content. In the navbar, a gold border is created over whichever page the user is currently on, to help with navigation. I've included two uses of javascript. One is the button to change the background color of the page, and two is a clickable image on the home page to enlarge it. Some other graphics I've included are a home page logo, favicon, personal logo, email logo, and an arrow in the footer indicating that the text next to it is a link to bring the user back to the top of the page. The text style I used for the 'h2' tags is fantasy, I believe it fits with the theme well. In the body, the font style is arial, which means that anything a 'p' tag will inherit that style.

Intro to JWST

The recently launched space telescope, JWST (James Webb Space Telescope), is equipped with some of the most advanced optic technology to date. The objective of this telescope is to discover the origins of life on other worlds and analyze the formation of the early universe. It is expected that this telescope will operate for about 20 years, plenty of time to discover so much about the universe. Currently as of writing this piece, the JWST is complete with its mirror alignment and is now fine tuning its instruments. Once this is finished, we will be able to see further than ever before! So come on, lets explore everything that we need to know about this fantastic technology.
